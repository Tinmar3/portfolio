Just a little bit about me...

Hello, I'm Martin Angjić.

I've been interested in web development since my student days at Zagreb University of Applied Sciences (TVZ) and later I got an opportunity to become a front-end developer. So, here I am, with more than two years of creating web pages and apps under my belt.

First of all, I consider myself a really good professional. Of course, I don’t mind having friends and good company at work, but professionalism should be number one.

Design slicing

My strongest weapon is creating front-end from mockup designs (aka PSD-to-HTML/slicing), but I don’t hesitate creating designs myself (like this page you are looking at), or cooperate with UI/UX designer in creating. I'm very good at CSS(3) and I am using JS only for more advanced stuff. All my pages and apps are mobile responsive, and as of later I am using total mobile first approach.

I like clean-cut and tenable code. To name everything with the right name and sort it in as many files as necessary. That’s why I like to use SCSS and Webpack. My colleagues know that if they want me in a good mood, inline element CSS code is not an option…

JavaScript development

Developing way which I enjoy the most is Vanilla (JS) (with the help of Webpack, Babel and some light libraries), or in ReactJS, depending on the project needs. I think external libraries should be used only if you really need them – eg. if I need simple modal screen, I won’t include whole JQuery, I’ll rather develop it myself and hence have more control of what’s happening. On the other side, of course I won’t be reinventing the wheel – I’ll use Zenscroll for scroll animation, or Dragdealer for dragging elements, and so on. If needed, I can use JQuery too, as I have a lot of experience with it.

Furthermore, I am good at programming. My final work at college was written in Java, and after that I used that language for a year as an Android developer. After learning Java I mastered JavaScript well too. Also, if needed, I can occasionally get my hands dirty at back-end, as I know programming paradigms.

My next move? To learn SVG and master it, and to start building projects with CSS Grid.

P.S.

At the end of the day, web and its appearance are my passion. I love beautiful web pages, cool animations and smooth transitions. Neat and wonderful pages, with clean-cut and wonderful code, make me one really happy developer.

In my free time, I’m doing fitness and running Chelsea Croatia Supporters' Club, which I’m vice-president of (and it counts about 250 members from around the Croatia!).
